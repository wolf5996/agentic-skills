# Editorial Guidelines for Recommendation Letters

These rules govern the content quality of every letter produced using the recommendation-letter workflow.

## Section Limits

- **Maximum 5 competency sections** plus Overall Assessment.
- **Mandatory headings:**
  1. Theoretical Knowledge
  2. Technical Proficiency
  3. Interpretation and Analytical Insight
  4. Independence, Initiative, and Professionalism
  5. Communication Skills
- Do not add optional sections (e.g. Interaction with Colleagues, Adaptability, Areas for Development, Time Period). These inflate length without adding distinctive evidence.
- Each competency section: **3-4 sentences maximum**.
- Overall Assessment: **2-3 sentences**.
- Total letter length: **2 pages maximum** (A4, 12pt, 25mm margins). When trimming, cut adjectives and redundant framing before removing examples.

## Specificity Over Superlatives

- Every competency section must include **at least one concrete example:** a specific dataset, project, finding, or problem solved.
- A section consisting entirely of adjective-driven praise is not acceptable.
- Cap superlatives (`exceptional`, `rare`, `exemplary`, `outstanding`, `remarkable`) to **maximum 2 per section.** Replace excess superlatives with specific evidence.
- When listing technical tools or methods, always pair them with what the person **built or produced** using them (e.g., "built a reproducible pipeline that processed 12,000 cells through QC, normalisation, and clustering").

## Repetition Control

- Never restate the same core claim across multiple sections.
- If "operates above their academic stage" appears once, do not rephrase it elsewhere.
- Distribute distinct evidence across sections rather than recycling the same general praise.

## Credibility Signals

- Include **at least one instance of growth, challenge overcome, or skill acquired** during the placement. Pure praise without a developmental arc reads as less credible.
- Quantify where possible: duration of supervision (e.g., "six-month period"), size of datasets ("12,000 cells"), number of pipelines built, or project milestones completed.

## Programme Tailoring

- Each competency section's **closing sentence must explicitly link the competency to the specific programme** being applied to. Use terminology from the target programme's description.
- This is a mandatory rule, not optional.
- The Overall Assessment should also restate programme fit briefly.

## Superlative Budget

Track and cap across the entire letter:
- **Per section:** max 2 superlatives.
- **Total letter:** aim for fewer than 10 superlatives overall. Preferate evidence and quantification.

## Formatting Constraints

- **No em dashes.** Use commas, parentheses, or separate sentences instead.
- Sign-off block: institutional affiliation and department only. No contact details unless explicitly requested.
- Use standard section headings only (listed above). No creative or renamed headings.

## Overall Assessment Mandates

- Always include the **top-X% benchmark** (e.g., "top 5% of all students and junior researchers I have supervised at his academic stage").
- Include a concise restatement of programme fit.
- Keep to 2-3 sentences total.

## Growth Arc Template

When framing a credibility signal, prefer this structure:
1. **Baseline:** State the skill level or challenge at the start of the placement.
2. **Action:** Describe what the applicant did independently to address it.
3. **Outcome:** State the concrete result or skill acquired.

Example: "Within the first month, Omar learned SCTransform independently after recognising that standard normalisation introduced unwanted variation, and he implemented the correction without daily prompting."
